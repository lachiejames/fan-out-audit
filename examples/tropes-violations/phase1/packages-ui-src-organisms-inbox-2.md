## MessageCard.tsx

**User-facing strings:**

- `"Reply suggestion"` — label above the AI-generated reply preview (line 268).
- `"Send"`, `"Edit"`, `"Dismiss"` — action buttons on reply suggestion (lines 282-309).
- `"AI suggested"` — small badge shown on highlighted messages (line 325).

### Violation: "Reply suggestion"

**File:** `packages/ui/src/organisms/inbox/MessageCard.tsx`, line 268

```tsx
<span className="font-medium text-foreground text-xs">Reply suggestion</span>
```

**Trope:** Hedged AI output label. "Reply suggestion" is deliberately soft — it avoids saying "AI-generated reply" or "Draft reply" because of the implied uncertainty. This hedging pattern appears throughout AI products to soften the blow if the reply is bad. It contributes to the "AI as uncertain assistant" trope.

**Severity:** Low. The softening is reasonable UX (don't over-promise), but "Reply suggestion" is generic enough that it sounds like boilerplate. Consider "Your draft" (more ownership), "AI draft" (more honest), or a platform-specific label.

### Violation: "AI suggested"

**File:** `packages/ui/src/organisms/inbox/MessageCard.tsx`, line 325

```tsx
<span ...>
  <Circle className="h-2.5 w-2.5" />
  AI suggested
</span>
```

**Trope:** Vague AI action label. "AI suggested" doesn't say what was suggested or why this message was surfaced. It's the kind of badge that signals "AI did something here" without explaining what or why. Compare to `"Needs reply"` or `"Reply ready"` which would tell users what to do.

**Severity:** Low-medium. Appears on highlighted message cards, visible whenever the inbox has highlights. The text is prominent enough to matter.

### Violation: "AI-generated reply"

**File:** `packages/ui/src/organisms/inbox/platform-views/GmailThreadView.tsx`, line 482

```tsx
<span>AI-generated reply</span>
```

**Trope:** Redundant AI attribution in UI. This label is shown inside the composer to indicate the content was AI-generated. While transparency is good, "AI-generated reply" is a system-internal descriptor rather than a user-benefit frame. The label explains provenance, not what the user should do with it. Compare: "SlopWeaver drafted this" (branded, specific), "Review before sending" (action-oriented), or just the Regenerate/Discard buttons without a label.

**Severity:** Low. Inside the composer, not a headline. But this pattern repeats across all three platform views (Gmail, Linear, Slack).
